7 Everyday Fruits to Keep the Doctor Away

Published: 25/11/2019

Illnesses and health problems have tremendous impact in our day to day lives. They greatly affect different aspects of our lives such productivity, mood, happiness and our psychological well-being.

I believe that anyone and everyone enjoys being in good health and not having to visit the doctor or take some medication just to get the body functioning properly. Although minor health problems such as a cold or fever may catch anyone from time to time due to factors such as change in weather, dust or exhaustion from working all day, more serious conditions may arise due to one's lifestyle and habits.

Consuming a balanced diet has become a cliché since every nutritionist and doctor will stress the importance of ensuring one consumes the vitamins, minerals, fats, and energy in the right proportions daily. However, a lot of people don't exactly know the complete composition of a balanced diet. According to WHO , the leading cause of diseases and illnesses is the habit people engage in consuming unhealthy diets and physical inactivity.

Tremendous research undertakings have been accomplished and ascertained that fruits offer optimum levels of vitamins, fiber, and other minerals for the Reference Daily Intake(RDI). To that end, doctors, nutritionists and health organizations recommend that for our daily food intake to be a balanced diet, it should be structured such that about a third include five portions of different fruits.

The fruits discussed below offer nearly all the doctor recommended vitamins and minerals for the RDI and should be an important part of your diet.

1. Apple

Apples provide a whole lot of minerals and vitamins. Apples provide the body about 15% of Vitamin C, 5% of Vitamins A, E, B1, B2, B6 & K.

To spice it up, they provide about 5g of Fiber, nearly 100g of Calories and research has shown that they still provide about 3% of copper, potassium and manganese, and has high calcium content. In addition, research has shown that apples possess tremendous weight loss properties as well as the ability to lower cholesterol levels.

The apple peel was discovered to contain half of the fiber as well as nutrients referred to as polyphenols. The high fiber content is associated with weight loss properties. Furthermore, the fiber is soluble which enhances the body’s ability to lower blood cholesterol levels. As a result, apples are linked to lowering the risk of suffering from heart diseases.

The polyphenols mentioned earlier possess antioxidant properties assisting the body in lowering blood pressure and reducing the risk of stroke by 20%. Apples have been discovered to lower risk of diabetes, mitigating the chances of cancer development as well as asthma. Extensive research has associated apples with good bone development and shielding the brain from mental deterioration. The high calcium content ensures that bones are healthy and strong reducing the risk of acquiring osteoporosiswhich makes bones fracture and break easily.


2. Grapefruit

Grapefruit provides the body with high levels of Vitamins A and C, essential minerals as well as energy. Since grapefruit is made up of about 90% water and high fiber content, it keeps the body hydrated, improves digestion and skin health. Grapefruits have also been found to lower cholesterol levels, insulin levels and proved to contain significant weight loss properties.

Research conducted by the American Heart Association showed that grapefruit is a huge source of flavonoids and potassium, which help address the heart and chronic problems. As such, daily intake grapefruit significantly lowers the risk of stroke, keeps blood pressure in check and promote heart health.

3. Blueberries

Another fruit that you ought to integrate into your daily diet are blueberries which come with a whole lot of health benefits from providing fiber, antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals such as manganese and copper.

Blueberries have been linked to improving insulin levels and reducing blood sugars. They possess anti-inflammatory properties which help in shielding against chronic and heart disease. Blackberries also contribute to lowering cholesterol levels thus preventing obesity.

Furthermore, the antioxidants play a fundamental role in promoting skin health and studies have confirmed that berries protect against the development of colon, breast and mouth cancer.


4. Pineapple

Though not as nutritious as apples, pineapples are also on another level providing over 100% of the RDI for Vitamin C. They contain enzymes that possess anti-inflammatory properties as well as the ability to increase the body’s resistance to tumor and cancer development.

Pineapples are also rich in minerals such as manganese-containing about 75% of its RDI and comes with loads of antioxidants. Research shows that pineapples have tremendeous contributions to the health of various body systems and functions. From promoting the immune system, bone and skin health, reducing the risk of asthma, improving digestion, to regulating blood pressure.

Preventing blood clot as well as some the growth of cancer cells are other crucial health benefits pineapples offer based on recent studies.

5. Watermelon

Doctors and nutritionists have emphasized that bodies require water to prevent dehydration which drains energy from the body. Females are advised to ensure an intake of about 11 cups and men 15 cups of water to keep the body systems functioning properly.

Water performs crucial roles in the body such as regulating temperatures, protective sensitive tissues plus getting reed of wastes and toxins. Moreover, blood is made mostly of water. Thus, it is evident that watermelon should be a fruit in our daily diets.

Watermelons are about 90% water, are rich in Vitamins A and C, as well as antioxidants that have been proved to have significant cancer-fighting properties. Lycopene which is one of the major antioxidant watermelons provide has been associated with reducing blood pressure and cholesterol levels. As such, heart health is promoted with the addition of watermelons in the diet and the body is kept hydrated since they are 90% water.


6. Avocado

If you are looking to keep your heart in perfect health, then you my friend must include avocadoes in your daily diet.

Doctors recommend intake of unsaturated fats that avocadoes provide in plenty contrary to the saturated fats that are found in cheese, butter and fatty meat. The healthy fats they offer provide about 30% of the RDI for potassium which research shows has tremendous benefits when it comes to lowering the risk of stroke by regulating the blood pressure.

Research has shown that people consume insufficient potassium below 3.5g while consuming high sodium acquired from the salt in our daily meals. Such practice gives rise to heart related health issues such as high blood pressure and stroke. Avocadoes are also an excellent source high fiber and magnesium content.

7. Orange

Oranges are one of the most consumed of the citrus fruits and it’s also one of the of the healthiest fruits with great health benefits. Oranges are rich in Vitamin C, fiber, potassium, manganese, choline among other essential minerals that improve the functioning of different body systems.

Adequate research has recorded the contribution of consuming oranges in helping combat serious health problems such as high blood pressure, reducing chances of heart disease, stroke, improving heart and skin health. Doctors have linked oranges with reducing the risk of the development of kidney stones and the ability to facilitate the absorption of iron in the body.

The American Journal of Epidemiology researched further and revealed that consumption of oranges at a tender age significantly reduces chances of developing childhood leukemia. That just shows how important an orange a day is just right from a young age.

Bottom Line...

Fruits have an integral role in ensuring that we are protected from many of the contemporary health issues and conditions that are the leading causes of the demise of millions across the globe. As such, to keep a clear conscience and be at peace knowing that you are healthy in every aspect, comes down to ensuring fruits become part of your daily diet.

As the above proven facts through scientific research indicate, the pursuit of the best defence against illnesses and diseases is making fruits part of our daily diet. Luckily, we are here to give you directions on how you can stay on top of your health and wellbeing.
